🥐Croissant🥐

Bread in the shape of a crescent moon was already known in classical antiquity. A pastry resembling the croissant is traditionally called Kipferl and originated in Austria; it is already mentioned in the 13th century. The croissant was introduced around 1839 by the Viennese businessman August Zang when he opened the Boulangerie Viennoise (Viennese bakery) on the Rue de Richelieu in Paris. Here he sold Viennese rolls and pastries, the so-called viennoiserie. He sold, among other things, the kipferl, which is seen as a precursor to the croissant. August Zang devised a variant of the kippferl, made from laminated dough (a type of puff pastry). Due to its shape, this was named the crescent moon by the Parisians and given the name croissant.
